# Leadership principles (Amazon LPs as a superset)

Amazon's 16 Leadership Principles (LPs) are the most exhaustive public articulation of what elite tech companies actually evaluate. Every other company's competency framework maps onto a subset of these. If you have crisp, evidence-backed stories for all 16, you are prepared for Google, Meta, Netflix, Stripe, and the rest.

Official reference: [Amazon Leadership Principles](https://www.amazon.jobs/content/en/our-workplace/leadership-principles)

---

## The 16 LPs — one-line meaning + story prompt

| # | Principle | What it really means | Story prompt |
|---|-----------|----------------------|--------------|
| 1 | **Customer Obsession** | Start with the user's actual problem, not what they asked for | Tell me about a time you pushed back on a stakeholder request because you believed it would harm the end user |
| 2 | **Ownership** | Act as if you own the outcome, even when it's outside your team | When did you go well beyond your job description to prevent or fix a serious problem? |
| 3 | **Invent and Simplify** | Find the boring obvious solution that everyone else overcomplicated | Describe a time you simplified a system, process, or API in a way that had outsized impact |
| 4 | **Are Right, A Lot** | Have strong judgment and update it with evidence | When were you confident you were right, got pushed back, held firm, and turned out to be correct? Also: when did you change your mind based on data? |
| 5 | **Learn and Be Curious** | Proactively learn adjacent domains, not just your stack | What's the last technical area you studied outside your immediate role, and how did it affect your work? |
| 6 | **Hire and Develop the Best** | Raise the bar with every hire; actively grow your team | Walk me through a candidate you championed who turned out excellent, or one you rejected despite pressure |
| 7 | **Insist on the Highest Standards** | Refuse to ship work you'd be embarrassed by | Describe a time you called out quality issues others were willing to live with |
| 8 | **Think Big** | Propose directional changes, not incremental tweaks | What is the largest-scope initiative you proposed? What was the vision, and how did you build support? |
| 9 | **Bias for Action** | Make reversible decisions fast; don't wait for perfect info | Tell me about a time you moved quickly on incomplete information and it paid off |
| 10 | **Frugality** | Achieve more with less; constraints breed creativity | When did you deliver something important with far fewer resources than you wanted? |
| 11 | **Earn Trust** | Be radically honest, including about bad news | When did you deliver a message — to leadership or a peer — that was uncomfortable but necessary? |
| 12 | **Dive Deep** | Stay connected to technical details even as you take broader scope | Tell me about a time your hands-on investigation uncovered something that would have been missed if you'd delegated it |
| 13 | **Have Backbone; Disagree and Commit** | Argue hard, then commit fully once the decision is made | When did you publicly disagree with a decision, lose, and still drive its execution wholeheartedly? |
| 14 | **Deliver Results** | Actually ship things; outcomes over intentions | Walk me through the most complex project you drove to completion despite serious obstacles |
| 15 | **Strive to be Earth's Best Employer** | Care concretely about your team's growth, safety, and wellbeing | What did you do to improve your team's working conditions or remove a systemic bloat? |
| 16 | **Success and Scale Bring Broad Responsibility** | Think about second-order effects on society, not just customers | When did you slow down or change a technical decision because of ethical, safety, or societal concerns? |

## What "bar raiser" means and how to prepare

Amazon Bar Raisers (BRs) are certified interviewers empowered to veto any hire. Every other company has an equivalent: the "calibration interviewer" at Google, the "skip-level shadow" at Meta, the "culture vote" at Stripe.

**What the BR is actually checking:**

- Are your stories **specific** (dates, numbers, team sizes, outcomes)?
- Do you use "I" instead of "we" for your own decisions?
- Is there a **counterfactual** — what would have happened without you?
- Do you demonstrate growth in scope over your career arc?

**Anti-patterns that fail the bar:**

- Describing team achievements without your specific lever
- Stories with no measurable outcome ("things improved")
- Scope that doesn't grow across your most recent 3 years
- Disagreement stories where you just went along to avoid conflict

**Script for injecting scope:** "At the time this was unusual for an IC because it normally required a VP sponsor. I drove it anyway by…"

## Adapting to other company frameworks

### Google — Googleyness + General Cognitive Ability (GCA)

Google's competencies map to LPs as follows:

| Google signal | Closest LP(s) |
|---------------|---------------|
| Googleyness / culture fit | Earn Trust, Hire and Develop, Strive to be Best Employer |
| GCA / structured problem solving | Are Right A Lot, Dive Deep |
| Leadership | Ownership, Think Big, Have Backbone |
| Role-related knowledge | Invent and Simplify, Insist on Standards |

**Key difference:** Google weights *how you reasoned* more than what you decided. After each LP story, add a "reasoning out loud" layer: "I chose this approach because I considered X and Y and believed X had better second-order properties because…"

### Meta — The four core signals

| Meta signal | Closest LP(s) |
|-------------|---------------|
| Move fast | Bias for Action, Deliver Results |
| Impact at scale | Think Big, Customer Obsession |
| Build social value | Success and Scale Bring Broad Responsibility |
| Be direct | Have Backbone, Earn Trust |

**Key difference:** Meta explicitly rewards "what would you do if this broke for 1 billion people." Frame stories at scale even if your direct exposure was smaller: "The principle I applied here would hold at 10x our user base because…"

### Netflix — Freedom and Responsibility culture

Netflix does not use a numbered LP list. Their culture memo emphasizes judgment over rules. Map your stories to:

- **Judgment**: Are Right A Lot + Dive Deep
- **Communication**: Earn Trust + Have Backbone
- **Impact**: Deliver Results + Think Big
- **Curiosity**: Learn and Be Curious

**Key difference:** Netflix heavily penalizes "I followed the process." Your stories must show you acted with discretion, not compliance. Reframe any process-following story: "The process existed but I recognized this case fell outside its assumptions, so I…"

## Structuring your answers — STARR (not STAR)

The standard STAR (Situation, Task, Action, Result) is table stakes. Add **R for Reflection** to distinguish Staff-level candidates.

```
S — Set the context in 2–3 sentences. Include the stakes.
T — State your specific responsibility (not the team's).
A — Describe your actions in first person, with forks in the road.
R — Quantify the result with a before/after delta if possible.
R — Reflect: what you'd do differently, or what you learned.
```

**Example opening (Own it immediately):**

> "In Q3 2024 I was the tech lead for a service handling 400M requests/day. We had a production incident that exposed a design flaw I had originally approved 18 months earlier. I want to walk you through what I did and what I wish I'd done differently."

This opening pre-empts the BR's "tell me what went wrong" follow-up and shows ownership and self-awareness simultaneously.

## Phrasing library — adapt verbatim

**Disagreement + commitment (LP 13):**
> "I told [VP name] I thought this approach would create technical debt we'd spend 18 months unwinding. I documented my concerns in writing so they were on record. The decision went the other way. From that point, I was the most vocal advocate for making it succeed — because half-hearted execution would prove nothing."

**Raising the bar on a hire (LP 6):**
> "The candidate was impressive but I voted no. My concern was that every example they gave was team-level, not individual. The hiring manager pushed back. I asked for one more interview focused specifically on their individual technical decisions. After that round, the hiring manager agreed with my assessment."

**Customer before feature (LP 1):**
> "The PM wanted a new export format. I asked to speak directly with three customers first. Two of them told me the real problem was that the existing export was slow, not missing. We shipped a 4x faster export in 2 weeks instead of a 6-week feature that would have missed the actual pain."

**Frugality under constraint (LP 10):**
> "I had no budget for new infrastructure. I spent two days profiling and found we were doing 12 redundant downstream calls per request. Removing those gave us 40% capacity headroom without a dollar spent."

## 30-day prep checklist

- [ ] Write codenames for your top 8 stories
- [ ] Map each story to its primary and secondary LPs
- [ ] Add one quantified outcome to every story
- [ ] Practice each story out loud — 2 minutes maximum
- [ ] Record yourself and check for "we" when you mean "I"
- [ ] Run one mock with a peer who will ask follow-up questions
- [ ] Read the [Amazon Leadership Principles](https://www.amazon.jobs/content/en/our-workplace/leadership-principles) page verbatim — memorize the titles
- [ ] Identify your two weakest LPs and create at least one story for each
