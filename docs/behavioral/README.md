# Behavioral & Leadership (Staff bar)

At Staff level the behavioral rounds carry real weight: they probe whether you've *operated* at
Staff scope, not just written good code. As AI absorbs implementation, communication, influence,
and judgment matter more.

## The four Staff signals — ✅ [`staff-signals.md`](staff-signals.md)
- [ ] **Direction-setting** — choosing which problems are worth solving; setting technical roadmap beyond your team
- [ ] **Influence without authority** — aligning other teams/PMs/leaders when they initially disagree
- [ ] **Navigating ambiguity** — turning vague business goals into concrete engineering strategy
- [ ] **Multiplier effect** — mentoring, raising standards, making others more effective

## Story bank (STARL) — ✅ [`story-bank.md`](story-bank.md) (template + worked example)
- [ ] Build **3–4 high-water-mark stories**, each reusable across themes. Format: **S**ituation,
  **T**ask, **A**ction, **R**esult, **L**earning. Emphasize *architectural decisions you owned*
  and *cross-team outcomes you drove* — not solo implementation.
- [ ] Map each story to multiple question themes (see below).

## Question banks by theme — ✅ [`question-banks.md`](question-banks.md)

## Why this matters
Interviewers look for evidence you *solved Staff-level problems before*. Concrete scope, measurable
impact, and the "I influenced N teams to…" shape of story beat polished but small implementation tales.
